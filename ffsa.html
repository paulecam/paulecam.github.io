French and Francophone Students Association (FFSA)

Last year, a good friend and I founded the French and Francophone Students Association (FFSA) at U of T’s St. George campus after realizing that no such club existed.
FFSA is a celebration of culture and community tied by language more than country, even though both my co-founder and I happen to be from France. Our goal is to give all francophones a space to speak their mother tongue, connect with others, and celebrate the incredible cultures and heritages tied to the French language.
This isn’t the most formal, rule-bound club you could join by design. It’s more like a group of friends who love organizing events and creating opportunities for others than a strictly structured organization.
We kicked things off last year with an amazing executive team and, in just five months, organized five events and grew to over 75 members. For the upcoming year, we’re looking forward to continuing to foster a strong francophone community at U of T through dinners, panels, collaborations, and a stronger online presence.
You can follow along with us at @ffsa.uoft on Instagram, and see some of the graphics I made for our events down below.

Après-ski

Après-ski was a potluck style event, where we celebrated the culture from Savoie and Haute-Savoie with a classic fondue dish. It was a very convivial evening, and a real success as one of our first big events, with over 40 attendees.

Galette des Rois

Later on, at the end of January, we celebrated the french tradition known as Galette des Rois. The holiday can be traced back to Ancient Rome, and consists in the sharing of a traditional pie in which is placed a figurine. The pie is cut, and shared amongst all that are present. Whoever gets the figurine is crowned King/Queen, and gets to choose a co-ruler. The event was, again, a real success, and a great moment of community.

Dîner au Paradis

Our last event of the year was a dinner organised in collaboration with Le Paradis, a restaurant in Toronto, ON. We shared a traditional french meal with exactly 30 attendees, to end the year on a good note.
