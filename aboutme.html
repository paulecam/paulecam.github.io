About me

As a short biography, I was born and raised in Lyon, a city in the south-east of France. I grew up between Lyon and the Alps, where my parents live now.
I’m the youngest child of five, and my unbiased opinion on my siblings is that they are all incredibly accomplished, amazing people.
My parents aren’t half-bad either. They are both science-oriented people, and I don’t remember a time I wasn’t intrigued and interested in matters of physics and mathematics.
The choice of the space domain was made when I was 14, and participated in a program offered by my middle school to learn the basics of aeronautics (and aerospace). After that first encounter, I moved to Canada at 18 years old to start a bachelor’s degree in theoretical astrophysics as a first stepping stone to a (hopefully) long career.

What that career entails exactly I have no clear idea yet.
Throughout my undergraduate, I was exposed to the wonderful and thrilling world of academia, and am determined to pursue my studies in graduate school to obtain a Masters degree, and eventually a doctorate. I’m also incredibly interested in the work being done in the space industry, with new space technologies, improvements on space medicine, and the future of space exploration.
I care deeply about the political and social challenges of our time, especially when it comes to the climate crisis. To know space is to understand the rarity of our planet, and how astounding it is that it even exists. I hope to be able to work in a field that is contributing to the global efforts for the environment, whether that’s directly through sustainable technologies and space applications, or indirectly by advancing our understanding of the Universe and reminding people just how precious our own planet is.

On a lighter note, with my free time I love to draw, and read, although I don’t do it as much as I’d want to; and I’m a better book purchaser than I am a reader. My favorite book is Les Misérables from Victor Hugo, unoriginal pick yet easily the most amazing book ever written. When I’m home in the mountains, I love to hike and bike. I usually love the outdoors, but I don’t really like cities. I love to write, and give my opinion, and talk. You’ll probably see a lot of that on here (eventually). There's not much more to say about me, this was my short biography!
